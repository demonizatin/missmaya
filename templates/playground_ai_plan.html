🧠 AI Plan — what gets saved & how it's classified

← Back to playground 📘 Full docs Prompts

What this is. Every time you finish a chat, the playground runs one merge LLM call that decides what — if anything — to save from the transcript. This page documents exactly what it puts where, with the rules and concrete examples. No magic.

Why it matters. Memory that's classified wrong propagates: a moment misfiled as a fact never expires; an event misfiled as a fact gets brought up out of context; a casual comment misfiled as a moment makes Maya treat it as emotionally weighted. The classification is the foundation.

Read the buckets in this order: facts → events → moments → mood → cooldown → lore → anticipation → skills.

Quick decision flow — when the merge LLM looks at one user statement

1. Did the user state it about themselves? If no (Maya invented it / it's about Maya / it's third-party gossip) → discard.
2. Is it a TIMELESS attribute (name, profession, hobby, family, aspiration)? → facts
3. Does it have a SPECIFIC DATE attached (exam, wedding, deadline, appointment)? → events (auto-archived 14 days after date)
4. Is it EMOTIONALLY WEIGHTED with persistent significance (fear, pride, ongoing strain, hope)? → moments
5. Did it land as a JOKE / shared reference / running bit between you and Maya? → lore
6. Was it a recurring English mistake or a notable correct usage? → skills.error_patterns / skills.wins
7. Is it none of the above? → discard. Default to silence over invention.

📒 facts timeless never expires

Things that are true about you and stay true. No date attached.

What goes here

  • Identity: name, nickname, profession, mother tongue, hometown, current city
  • Family: spouse, parents, siblings, kids — and named people in your life ("my boss Karthik", "sister Riya")
  • Background: where you grew up, schools, formative experiences ("broke leg playing cricket at 16")
  • Interests & hobbies (recurring, not one-off): cricket, cooking, photography
  • Aspirations: "want to do an MBA someday", "want to live abroad"
  • English profile: "comfortable with daily talk, nervous about formal speaking"

How it's stored

  • Scalar fields get overwritten only on explicit contradiction (profession changes, mother tongue corrected). Identity field name is canonical and CANNOT be overwritten — attempts get redirected to nickname.
  • List fields (interests, background, family) get appended; deduped; never trimmed.

"I broke my leg playing cricket at 16."factsbackground += ["broke leg playing cricket at 16"]
"I'm having a bad day."— (NOT facts)not timeless → moments or skipped

📅 events date-bound auto-archive 14 days after the date

Specific things tied to a calendar date. Maya can ask about them as they approach or check in afterward.

What goes here

  • Exams, interviews, deadlines, appointments, tests
  • Weddings, parties, family gatherings, trips, travel dates
  • Promotions, milestones, anniversaries (one-off — recurring annual events are an open V6 issue)
  • Anything with a YYYY-MM-DD answer to "when?"

How it's stored

  • Required fields: what (short description) + date (resolved to YYYY-MM-DD even if you said "tomorrow")
  • Tracking flags: followed_up (Maya already asked about it in past tense — don't re-raise), added (when it entered memory)
  • Auto-archive: 14 days after the event date, the event is dropped. So "exam on May 4" disappears around May 18.

💭 moments emotional thread long-term

Emotionally weighted statements you've shared. No date. These shape Maya's tone — but she NEVER quotes them back.

What goes here

  • Fears: "I'm scared of group calls", "I freeze when my boss looks at me"
  • Prides: "I'm proud I finished the marathon", "I learned to drive last year, that was a big deal"
  • Ongoing strains: "I always feel behind my brother", "my work-life balance is rough"
  • Hopes / longings: "I miss my parents", "I want to feel less anxious in meetings"
  • Moments of vulnerability: "I cried during the movie last weekend"

How it's stored

  • Each entry: text, tone (anxious / sad / proud / scared / hopeful / frustrated / content / lonely / grateful / excited / neutral), sensitive (boolean), first_seen, last_seen, mentions (counter, bumps on repeat)
  • Sensitive flag = TRUE for acute disclosures or things you said not to bring up. Sensitive moments are stored but Maya is HARD-RULED to never raise them unprompted (Rule 29).
  • Dedup: same text seen again = mentions counter bumps; last_seen updates.

Examples

You sayBucketSaved as
"I get nervous on group calls at work."moments{text: "nervous on group calls", tone: "anxious", sensitive: false}
"I'm really proud I ran my first 10K last month."moments{text: "ran first 10K", tone: "proud"}
"I feel like a failure compared to my brother."moments{tone: "frustrated", sensitive: true}
"I miss my mom."moments{tone: "sad", sensitive: true}
"This biryani was so spicy, my stomach is crying."— (NOT a moment)casual / momentary, no persistent significance → discard or potentially lore

📊 mood_log session-level last 14 sessions kept

One emotional reading per session. Drives "how should Maya open today" — never spoken back at the user.

What goes here

  • One entry per session, written at end-of-chat by the merge LLM after seeing the whole transcript.
  • Label: low · anxious · neutral · content · energetic · no_read (LLM couldn't tell)
  • Energy: 1–10 integer (omitted if no_read)
  • Confidence: 0.0–1.0, the LLM's self-assessment
  • Behavioral: deterministic engagement_score from your message length, exclamations, negation density, etc. — used to cross-check the LLM's read
  • Agreement: match · soft_conflict · hard_conflict — final confidence is dampened on conflict
  • Linked event: optional reference to an event in the events bucket, if mood is clearly tied to it

How it's used (not displayed back to you)

  • Renders as a 5-step trajectory: low(4) → anxious(4)? → neutral(5) → content(7) → content(7)
  • Trailing ? = low confidence; the model de-weights that entry
  • Auto-detected hints: ⚠ sharp drop · 📉 trending down · 📈 trending up · ➖ persistent tier (this is your normal)
  • Baseline computed from last 14 entries (mode label + median energy) — Maya reads trajectory against your baseline, not against zero
  • Cadence: how often you've been chatting (daily / regular / sporadic / occasional)

🚫 cooldown repetition control last ~12 topics, last 10 openers

What Maya RAISED in recent sessions, so she doesn't repeat herself.

recent_topics (last 12 distinct)

  • Every distinct topic Maya brought up unprompted, with last_session, last_date, uses counter
  • Renders as the 🚫 OFF-LIMITS denylist at the very TOP of the prompt
  • Rule 32 makes these FORBIDDEN as Maya's opener for that session

  • One of: event_followup · moment_followup · skill_celebration · lore_callback · energy_match · general
  • Maya sees the recent sequence ("general → general → general") and is told to vary the kind

🎭 lore inside jokes up to 30 kept, decay by recency

Running jokes and shared references between you and Maya. Used sparingly for warmth.

What goes here

  • A phrase you repeat that becomes a tag ("going market", "the lehenga drama")
  • A teasing exchange that both sides enjoyed
  • A shared reaction or bit that returned

How it's stored

  • Each entry: id, what (short label), context (1-2 sentences for future-Maya to wield), first_seen, last_used, uses counter
  • Rule 33 caps usage at 1 lore callback per session, never the same item twice in a row
  • Lore dormant for ≥20 days enters the anticipation queue as a possible callback ("she didn't forget, she was waiting")

✅ anticipation_queue recomputed every save 5 items max · 14-day TTL

Pre-loaded specific opener candidates for your NEXT session. Built deterministically — no LLM call.

How it's built

Rebuilt every save by pg_compute_anticipation_queue(). Sources, in priority order:

  1. Events in [-3d, +7d] window — closer = higher priority. Just-passed events get followup; upcoming get attention.
  2. Recent non-sensitive moments (≤21 days) — anxious/sad tones get a priority boost.
  3. Dormant lore (≥20 days unused) — older lore gets a small bonus. Surprise callbacks.
  4. Recent skill wins (≤7 days) — for celebration.

Sorted by priority, deduped by source_id, capped at 5. Items expire 14 days after computation.

Rule 31 — primary opener source

  • If any item has priority ≥ 5, Maya MUST open with one of them
  • Pick exactly ONE; weave it in naturally; never recite the queue itself
  • Falls back to fresh angle only if queue is empty or all items are below threshold

🎯 skills tutoring intelligence errors: 20 active · wins: 30 max

Recurring English mistakes you make, and notable correct usages. Drives Rule 28 corrections + Rule 34 celebrations.

error_patterns

  • Each entry: pattern (the GENERIC rule, not the literal sentence), examples (last 5 literal user lines), occurrences, first_seen, last_seen, status (active / fixed)
  • Same pattern across sessions auto-merges with bumped occurrences
  • LLM can mark a pattern as fixed if you consistently produce the correct form

wins

  • Each entry: what (the skill — e.g. "used past perfect correctly"), examples (last 5 literal user lines), count, dates
  • Counter bumps on repeat correct use
  • Rule 34 has a HARD GATE: Maya can ONLY celebrate if she can quote a literal example — no fabrication

Examples

You sayBucketSaved as
"I am going market for shopping." (drops "to")skills.error_patterns{pattern: "drops 'to' before destination", examples: ["I am going market for shopping"], occurrences: +1}
"I am having a doubt about this." (Indian-English "doubt" for "question")skills.error_patterns{pattern: "uses 'doubt' for 'question'"}
"I had finished the work by 5 yesterday." (correct past perfect)skills.wins{what: "used past perfect correctly", examples: ["I had finished the work by 5 yesterday"]}
A typo like "teh" instead of "the"— (NOT skills)typo, not a knowledge gap → discard

🚷 What we NEVER save

  • Anything Maya invented. If you didn't say it, it doesn't get persisted. Rule of "fact fidelity": only the user's own statements about themselves count.
  • Third-party gossip about specific real people you didn't name. Family member roles fine ("my mom is a teacher"); detailed claims about non-named third parties skipped.
  • Acute crisis content beyond a single sensitive flag. If you describe a harm-to-self situation, Maya offers Indian crisis support resources (iCall, Vandrevala) once, doesn't probe, doesn't persist details. Rule 21.
  • Verbatim language details that aren't yours. If you said "you sound stiff in your speech", that's about Maya's perception of you — moments captures the FEELING, not the literal phrase you used.
  • Fillers + small talk. "ok", "yes", "thanks", "lol" — discarded.
  • Things you've explicitly asked to be forgotten. Use "forget that I said X" — Maya removes it on next save (V4 work, currently relies on user clearing memory wholesale via the 🗑 button).
